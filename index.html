Dr. Jonas Salk

The man who stopped polio.

Dr. Jonas Salk and the polio vaccine

Dr. Jonas Salk started his fight against polio virus in 1947. It took him five years to find a cure. Being a head of Virus Research Lab at the University of Pittsburgh, he created a vaccine that could save many lives. He and his family were first volounteers to test this vaccine out.

There is hope in dreams, imagination, and in the courage of those who wish to make those dreams a reality.
Dr. Jonas Salk.

The first test succeed, and in April 1954 the new vaccine was tested nationwide within the United States. Results were impressive: number of cases of polio decreased from 29,000 in 1955 to less than 6,000 two years later.

To make his invetion available to everyone, Dr. Jonas Salk never patented his polio vaccine.

Thanks to Dr. Jonas Salk cases of polio are very rare now in many countries all arount the world. According to The World Health Organization, the decease could completely disappear in the near future.

The whole life of Dr. Jonas Salk was dedicated to help humanity. After defeating the polio virus, he was searching for a way to cure AIDS.

What is Polio?

Polio (also known as poliomyelitis) is a highly contagious disease caused by a virus that attacks the nervous system. Children younger than 5 years old are more likely to contract the virus than any other group. Polio attacks the nerve cells and sometimes the central nervous system, which can cause paralysis or even death.
